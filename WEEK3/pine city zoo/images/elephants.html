ANIMALS MAP PLACES

ELEPHANT

Elephants are the largest land animals on Earth, known for their intelligence, strong social bonds, and remarkable memory. They belong to the family Elephantidae, which includes three species:

African Savanna Elephant (Loxodonta africana) the largest species, found in open plains and woodlands

African Forest Elephant (Loxodonta cyclotis) smaller and more elusive, living in dense forests of Central and West Africa

Asian Elephant (Elephas maximus) slightly smaller than their African counterparts, with smaller ears and found in India, Southeast Asia, and Sri Lanka.

Elephants play a crucial role in ecosystems by shaping landscapes, dispersing seeds, and creating water sources used by other animals. Their intelligence rivals that of primates, with problem-solving abilities, self-awareness, and deep emotional connections within their herds.
