LION

The lion (Panthera leo) is one of the most iconic and powerful big cats, known for its majestic mane and social behavior. Lions live in prides, which consist of a group of females, their cubs, and a few dominant males.

They are apex predators, primarily found in the grasslands and savannas of Africa, though a small population still exists in India s Gir Forest.

Lions have roamed the Earth for hundreds of thousands of years. Fossil evidence traces their ancestry back over 2 million years, with lions once inhabiting Europe, Asia, and North America.

They were widespread during the Pleistocene epoch but eventually disappeared from many regions due to climate changes and human activity.

In ancient cultures, lions symbolized strength, courage, and royalty. They were featured in Egyptian mythology, depicted in Mesopotamian art, and revered in medieval European heraldry.
