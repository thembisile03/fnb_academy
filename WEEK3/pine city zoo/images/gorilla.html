GORILLA

Gorillas are the largest living primates and are closely related to humans, sharing about 96,99% of our DNA.

They are primarily herbivorous and inhabit the tropical forests of equatorial Africa.

Gorillas are divided into two species: the eastern gorilla and the western gorilla, with several subspecies.

Historically, gorillas were first documented by Western scientists in the 19th century, though indigenous African communities had long known about them.

The name "gorilla" comes from a Carthaginian explorer's account of a tribe of hairy people, recorded over 2,500 years ago

Gorillas evolved from a common ancestor shared with humans and chimpanzees, diverging around 7,11 million years ago.
